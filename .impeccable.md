## Design Context

### Users
**Customers** are homeowners or renters who need maintenance services (plumbing, electrical, cleaning, etc.). They're typically busy, may be stressed about a home issue, and want to book a reliable professional quickly with minimal friction. They browse categories, compare technicians, and track service progress.

**Technicians** are skilled tradespeople managing their availability, responding to job requests, and building their reputation. They need a fast, clear interface to manage their workflow on the go — often on job sites with limited attention.

Both audiences value speed, clarity, and trustworthiness above all.

### Brand Personality
**Professional, Clean, Efficient.**

FixIt positions itself as the competent, no-nonsense home maintenance platform. The brand communicates reliability without being cold — it's polished and structured but still approachable. Think of a well-organized professional who also happens to be easy to talk to.

**Emotional goals:** Trust & calm (users feel safe and in control), speed & efficiency (minimal friction), delight & ease (pleasantly simple), confidence & clarity (informed at every step with clear next actions).

### Aesthetic Direction
**Polished & structured** — clear visual hierarchy, subtle depth through shadows and cards, balanced information density. Never cluttered, never sparse to the point of feeling empty.

**Visual tone:** Modern mobile-native app feel. Card-based layouts, well-defined sections, generous but purposeful spacing.

**References:**
- **Uber / Uber Eats** — clean booking flows, real-time status tracking, professional yet accessible interface patterns
- **TaskRabbit / Thumbtack** — service marketplace browsing, category discovery, provider profiles with trust signals

**Anti-references:**
- No cluttered or busy interfaces — avoid dense dashboards, cramped layouts, or information overload
- No generic or template-like aesthetics — should feel custom and distinctive, not like a Bootstrap/Material starter kit

### Design Principles

1. **Clarity over cleverness** — Every screen should communicate its purpose instantly. Prioritize legible hierarchy, obvious CTAs, and unambiguous states over novel interactions.

2. **Structured breathing room** — Generous spacing guided by purpose, not decoration. Use the established 20px horizontal / 24px vertical rhythm. White space is a feature, not waste.

3. **Trust through polish** — Subtle shadows, consistent radii (10px base), smooth animations, and precise alignment signal quality. Details compound into confidence.

4. **Speed is a feature** — Minimize steps, reduce cognitive load, and make common paths feel instant. Both customers and technicians are busy — respect their time.

5. **Consistent but not monotonous** — Follow the design token system religiously (colors from `Colors`, classes from Tailwind config), but use the category color palette and contextual accents to keep interfaces visually engaging.

### Technical Design Tokens

| Token | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Primary** | `#036ded` (brand blue) |

| **Base radius** | 0.625rem (10px) |

### Accessibility
Standard best practices: good contrast ratios, proper accessibility labels, reasonable touch targets (min 40x40px), logical focus order. No formal WCAG certification target, but aim for AA-level contrast on all text as a baseline.
